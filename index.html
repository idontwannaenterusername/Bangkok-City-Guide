Attractions — Grand Palace, Temples & River

Best time: early morning to avoid crowds. Tickets & dress code info included.

Grand Palace

Opulent complex; dress modestly (no shorts, shoulders covered). Arrive early.

Wat Pho

Home of the Reclining Buddha and traditional Thai massage school.

Wat Arun

Riverside temple with iconic spire — beautiful at sunset across the river.

Food & Nightlife

From street food to rooftop views — what to try and where to go.

Chinatown (Yaowarat)

Nighttime food heaven: seafood stalls, dim sum, and sweet treats.

Khao San Road

Backpacker area with lively bars, cheap eats, and street performances.

Rooftop Bars

Sky-high views: book in advance for sunset (e.g., Vertigo, Sky Bar).

Where to stay

Neighborhood quick guide: Sukhumvit, Silom, Riverside, and Siam.

Sukhumvit

Good nightlife, restaurants, and BTS access — mid- to upscale hotels.

Riverside

More relaxed and scenic; perfect for riverboat access and romantic stays.

Silom

Central business district with a mix of night markets and calm pockets.

Transport & Getting Around

BTS/MRT for traffic-free travel, riverboats for sightseeing, and tuk-tuks for short trips.

BTS Skytrain & MRT

Fast & safe — use a Rabbit card or single-trip tokens. Peak hours get crowded.

Chao Phraya River Boats

Cheap and scenic — orange/green boats stop at major piers by the temples.

Tuk-tuks & Taxis

Negotiate tuk-tuk prices first; insist on the taxi meter for taxis.

Practical Tips

Money, safety, cultural notes, and useful apps.

Currency & Money

Carry small bills for markets. ATMs are widely available but watch fees.

Cultural Etiquette

Respect temple dress codes; remove shoes when entering some buildings.

Useful Apps

Download Grab (ride-hail) and a BTS/MRT map app. Stay hydrated in the heat.

About Bangkok

A brief introduction to the vibrant capital of Thailand.

The City of Angels

Bangkok, known in Thai as Krung Thep Maha Nakhon, is a city of contrasts with action at every turn. Marvel at the gleaming temples, catch a tuk-tuk along the bustling Chinatown, or take a long-tail boat through floating markets. Food is another Bangkok highlight, from local dishes served at humble street stalls to haute cuisine at romantic rooftop restaurants.

Culture and History

The heart of Bangkok is the Rattanakosin Island, where you'll find the Grand Palace, Wat Pho, and Wat Arun. The city's rich cultural heritage is visible in its magnificent temples, traditional dance performances, and the everyday life of its residents. Respect for the monarchy and religion is deeply ingrained in Thai culture.
